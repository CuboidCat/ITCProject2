The History of Krita

1999 Krita was initially conceived as part of the KOffice suite of applications, developed by the KDE community.
2005 The first official release of Krita was made available, focused on digital painting, drawing, and concept art creation.
2009 Krita became a standalone application, no longer tied to the KOffice suite, and was released as open-source software under the GPL license.
2015 Krita's first major milestone, Krita 2.9, was released with improvements in usability, performance, and new features aimed at professional digital artists.
2020 Krita 4.2 was released, adding powerful tools for animation, improved brush engines, and enhanced support for digital painting workflows.

Today, Krita is one of the most popular open-source digital painting tools, widely used by illustrators, concept artists, and animators. With its powerful features, extensive brush library, and professional-grade tools, Krita is a favorite in both the professional and hobbyist art communities.
